Panoramas

Warriors Pass

The view from Harkness lake up to the mountains at Warrior Pass is simply stunning. The lake is surrounded by lush pine forest. The mountains rise almost vertically from the foothills. Seven of the peaks are above 2500 metres. The highest peak, Blackrock, is 2789 metres. There are great photographs to be had from almost any angle. Especially stunning are night time shots taken from Harkness lake at dusk or dawn. At this point, the mountains appear purple of dark blue against the orange skyline.

Madison Ox Bow

Madison Ox Bow is the most perfect example of an ox bow lake in the final stages of formation. It has a unique perfect D profile found only in a handful of such geographical features in the world. There is a woodland covered island in the centre of the ox bow. On the island are several strategic viewpoints for great photography. On the south side there are excellent views along the river towards Granite Peak. For those interested in wildlife, beavers have made their lodges north of the Madison Ox Bow.

Chromatic Lakes

Perhaps the most enduring image of Greenstone parks is the sight of the multi-colored lakes in Devoir Valley known as the Chromatic Lakes. They are truly spectacular, and offer the photographer a chance to capture one of the planets unique geographical features. There are seven lakes. To the north of the lakes is a viewing tower. From here, you can get a bird's eye view of the rich range of colors that each lake exhibits. Visitors are not allowed with 500 metres of the lake due to the sulfur fumes given off by the water.

Tecumseh Valley

Unspoilt. Almost Uninhabited. The valley covers 200 square miles from Tulsoc in the north to Sioux River in the south. There are miles and miles of serene forest, majestic lakes, rolling hills and meandering rivers. The opportunities for superb photography are endless. Because of the size of this feature of this park, many of the best photo opportunities require significant hiking to get to. But no need to worry about carrying spare batteries. Each designated panorama point has its own solar charging unit.

Boult River Valley

Imagine you are one of the first settlers in this country. Imagine a land unspoilt by civilization. You need look no further than the Boult River Valley. This wonder of nature follows the Boult River a hundred miles north from Highway 43 to the foot of the Animus Peaks. There are serene stretches in the lowlands. Further north, you will encounter fast moving stretches and wild rapids. Flanked on both sides by forest interspersed with meadow, you cannot fail to find the perfect shot, especially when travelling by Kayak.

Ravens Range

A sheltered plateau set between a circle of imposing mountains in the heart of the Park. Because of its unique altitude and location, the plateau has its own unique micro-climate. It is superb for night time and evening photography, producing over the years some of the most iconic images of the park. Access to the best panoramas is by trail only. You need to be prepared for at least a two-day hike to get to the best panorama at Giant's Boulder. Proper kit and spare battery pack is essential.

Lobo Canyon

Named after the wolf pack that make this area their home, you cannot fail to come away from this visual treasure with anything but stunning shots. There are thirty-two designated panorama points, all marked on the official park map. Some of them require significant hiking and climbing to get to and there are no facilities for buying gear or charging equipment, so go well-stocked with batteries or a solar charger. And make sure you leave your details and your planned route at the Park Office before you begin your trip.

Old Unfaithful

You cannot come to Greenstone without getting some shots of the iconic Old Unfaithful geyser. Surpassed only in fame by the Yellowstone geysers, Greenstone offers some excellent opportunities to hone you moving image photography. Old unfaithful can be quiet for hours, then erupt in a torrent of boiling water and steam that takes the breath away. Surely one of nature's true wonders. There are two designated panorama points, and for safety reasons, you are asked not to go beyond these points at any time.

Salters Flats

Salters Flats is a unique confluence of rivers surrounded by overflow lakes. It sits on top of one of the parks thermal hot spots. Salt and other minerals leech up from the bedrock into the lakes, producing a range of visually stunning colors not found anywhere else in the world. Some of the photography produced here is simply stunning. Even beginners cannot fail to come up with beautiful images. There are four designated panorama points with charging points for electronics. You are advised not to bathe in or drink the water.
